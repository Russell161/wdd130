"World Famous" Ohio State Buckeyes

Ohio State football's inaugural season was in 1904. Ohio State ended the season with a 6-5 record as one of the sports few independent teams. 9 years later, Ohio State joined the then Western conference and 31 years after that, the Big Ten. Ohio State's vaunted history includes 40 conference championships, 8 national chamionships, and 7 Heisman Trophy winners. The prestigious history and yearly expectations of greatness are felt ar every level of the organization, from the front office to the largest fan base in the country.

Ohio Stadium - "The Horseshoe"

Ohio Stadium is also known as "The House that Harley Built" or more famously, "The Hourseshoe". Many fans affectionally refer to the stadium simply as "the shoe". With a capacity of over 100,000 people, its the 4th largest in the country and one of the largest in the world. A special tradition that takes place before each kickoff, people in the stadium take off a shoe and jump around to a particular song. Designed to get the croud loud, it caters to a riotous fanbase and to the nickname of the stadium.
